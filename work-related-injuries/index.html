Common Workplace Injuries We Treat

A physical therapist can evaluate and treat a wide range of work-related injuries, including:

Back Pain

Lifting injuries, strains, sciatica, and disc issues

Neck Pain

Cervical strains, tension, posture-related pain

Shoulder Injuries

Rotator cuff strains, impingement, repetitive motion injuries

Knee Injuries

Ligament sprains, meniscus injuries, joint pain

Carpal Tunnel Syndrome

Wrist and hand pain, numbness, tingling

Tendinitis

Elbow, wrist, and shoulder overuse conditions

What to Expect at Physical Therapy

Once a patient is referred to physical therapy with a work-related injury, the therapist will obtain important information about the injury, the patient's medical history, and pre-existing problems. Your evaluation may include assessment of:

Your History

Detailed review of how the injury occurred and relevant medical background

Range of Motion

Measuring how well your joints move and where restrictions exist

Pain Levels

Understanding what activities trigger or relieve your symptoms

Functional Limitations

Identifying specific work tasks that are currently difficult

Getting Back to Work

The ultimate goal of any treatment for work-related injuries is returning the patient to their prior level of function at work. The physical therapist will discuss specific job demands with the patient, educate them regarding the healing process, and develop a plan to safely progress back to full duty.

Your Treatment Plan May Include

Therapeutic Exercise

Strengthening and flexibility exercises tailored to your job demands

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques to reduce pain and restore mobility

Ergonomic Education

Guidance on proper body mechanics and workstation setup

Return-to-Work Planning

Progressive program to safely transition back to full work duties

When to Seek Help

If you've been injured on the job, early physical therapy intervention can help reduce pain, speed recovery, and get you back to work sooner. We accept workers' compensation cases.
