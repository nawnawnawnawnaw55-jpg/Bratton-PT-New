Sprains and Strains

Sprains and strains are common injuries that we sustain with daily activities, recreation, and competitive sports. But what is a sprain and what is a strain?

Strains

A strain is the tearing of muscle fibers. Muscle fibers are the individual units that make up your muscles. When you call upon the muscle to perform strong or repeated contractions, you may suffer a strain. Strains are classified into grades I, II, and III (mild, moderate, and severe).

Grade I (Mild)

Mild disruption of muscle fibers. There may be mild swelling, mild tenderness, and it may be painful to stretch and use the injured muscle.

Grade II (Moderate)

Moderate muscle fiber damage. Pain is moderate to severe, it definitely hurts to use and stretch the injured muscle, and bruising may be present.

Grade III (Severe)

Complete tearing of a muscle into two pieces, or separation of the muscle from its tendon. Severe pain, complete loss of strength, swelling, and bruising. Medical intervention is usually necessary.

Sprains

Sprains are tears of the ligaments and capsules (tissues around joints). Ligaments are made up of bundles of somewhat elastic, collagen tissue and attach one bone to another. Like strains, sprains are classified as grade I, II, or III in their severity.

  • ▸ Grade I: Minimal ligament fiber involvement — pain, swelling, mild loss of function
  • ▸ Grade II: Moderate or partial ligament tear — moderate to severe pain, swelling, bruising, loss of joint function
  • ▸ Grade III: Complete tearing of the ligament or joint capsule — severe pain, swelling, bruising, loss of function; orthopaedic intervention may be necessary

Tendinitis and Bursitis

Tendinitis and bursitis are two common conditions that affect the tendons and bursae in our body. These conditions can cause pain, inflammation, and limited range of motion, making it difficult to perform everyday activities.

What is Tendinitis?

Tendinitis is the inflammation of a tendon, which is a fibrous cord that attaches muscles to bones. It can occur in any tendon of the body but is most common in the shoulder, elbow, wrist, knee, and ankle. Repetitive motions, overuse, and poor posture are common causes. Symptoms include pain, swelling, and limited mobility of the affected joint.

What is Bursitis?

Bursitis is the inflammation of a bursa, which is a small fluid-filled sac that acts as a cushion between bones, tendons, and muscles. It can occur in any bursa of the body but is most common in the shoulder, elbow, hip, and knee. Like tendinitis, repetitive motions, overuse, and trauma are common causes.

How Physical Therapy Helps

Physical therapy can help manage tendinitis and bursitis by reducing pain and inflammation, improving range of motion, and restoring function. We assess your condition and develop a personalized treatment plan that may include:

  • ✓ Exercise Therapy — exercises to strengthen affected muscles, improve flexibility, and restore range of motion
  • ✓ Manual Therapy — hands-on manipulation to reduce pain, inflammation, and improve mobility
  • ✓ Modalities — ice/heat therapy, laser therapy, shock wave therapy, or electrical stimulation
  • ✓ Education — guidance on preventing re-injury and maintaining long-term health

When to Seek Help

If you are experiencing pain, swelling, or limited range of motion in any joint, consult with a physical therapist for an evaluation. Early intervention can help prevent further damage and improve your quality of life.
