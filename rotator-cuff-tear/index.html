What Is the Rotator Cuff?

The rotator cuff is a group of four muscles and tendons that stabilize your shoulder and help you lift and rotate your arm. These tendons keep the upper arm bone securely in the shoulder socket.

What Is a Rotator Cuff Tear?

A rotator cuff tear occurs when one or more of these tendons become damaged or torn. This can happen suddenly (acute injury) or develop over time (degenerative tear).

Common causes include:

  • ✓ Lifting something too heavy
  • ✓ Repetitive overhead movements
  • ✓ Falls or sudden injuries
  • ✓ Age-related wear and tear

Signs and Symptoms

  • ▸ Shoulder pain, especially when lifting or lowering your arm
  • ▸ Weakness in the shoulder
  • ▸ Difficulty reaching overhead or behind your back
  • ▸ Pain that worsens at night or when lying on the affected side

How Physical Therapy Helps

Pain Relief

  • ✓ Gentle movements to reduce stiffness
  • ✓ Modalities like ice, heat, or electrical stimulation

Restoring Mobility

  • ✓ Stretching exercises to improve range of motion
  • ✓ Joint mobilization techniques

Strengthening the Shoulder

  • ✓ Targeted exercises for rotator cuff and surrounding muscles
  • ✓ Gradual progression to rebuild strength safely

Improving Function

  • ✓ Training for daily activities (reaching, lifting, dressing)
  • ✓ Guidance on proper movement and posture

Benefits of Physical Therapy

  • ✓ Reduces pain without medication
  • ✓ Improves strength and flexibility
  • ✓ Helps avoid or recover from surgery
  • ✓ Supports long-term shoulder health

When to Seek Help

If shoulder pain is limiting your movement or not improving with rest, physical therapy can help you recover safely and effectively.
