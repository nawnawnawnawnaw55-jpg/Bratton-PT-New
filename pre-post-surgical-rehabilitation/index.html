Pre & Post-Surgical Rehabilitation

Surgeons routinely refer post-surgical patients to our physical therapists to help them recover strength, function, and mobility. We provide one-on-one, evidence-based treatment to facilitate your recovery and return to daily life.

Post-Operative Procedures We Treat

The rehabilitative process depends on the injury and type of surgery. We help patients recover and rehabilitate from a variety of orthopedic post-operative procedures, including:

  • ✓ Knee, hip and shoulder joint replacements
  • ✓ ACL, MCL, PCL, and LCL reconstruction
  • ✓ Arthroscopic knee surgeries (meniscectomies, meniscal repairs)
  • ✓ Ligament and tendon repair (including Achilles)
  • ✓ Arthroscopic hip surgeries (labral repairs)
  • ✓ Nerve releases (cubital, radial, carpal, tarsal tunnel)
  • ✓ Neck and back surgery (fusions, laminectomy, discectomy)
  • ✓ Bone or joint fusion (arthrodesis)
  • ✓ Tendon transfers
  • ✓ Surgical fixation of fractures (ORIF)
  • ✓ Rotator cuff and labral tear repairs
  • ✓ SLAP tear/lesion and shoulder arthroscopy

Therapies We Use

To help you regain movement and reduce pain, swelling, and stiffness, we may use one or more of the following:

Manual Therapy

Hands-on techniques to mobilize joints and soft tissues

Joint Mobilization

Gentle movements to restore normal joint mechanics

Muscle Energy

Active techniques to improve flexibility and alignment

Gait & Balance Training

Improving walking patterns and stability

TENS & Electrical Stimulation

Electrical muscle and nerve stimulation for pain relief

Therapeutic Exercise

Functional strength training tailored to your needs

Ultrasound

Deep heating to promote tissue healing

Functional Re-Education

Exercises to increase strength, endurance, motion, balance, and coordination

When to Seek Help

Whether you're preparing for surgery or recovering from one, physical therapy can significantly improve your outcomes. We work closely with your surgeon to ensure the best possible recovery.
